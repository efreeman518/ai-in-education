## For Educators

### Lesson Planning & Content Creation

AI excels at rapidly generating first drafts — outlines, rubrics, discussion questions, and reading lists — that you then refine and own.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Create a 50-minute lesson plan on [topic] for 9th graders. Include learning objectives, warm-up activity, main instruction block, and exit ticket."`
- `"Generate 10 discussion questions on [text/topic] ranging from factual recall to open-ended analysis."`
- `"Write a rubric for a persuasive essay assignment. Criteria: thesis clarity, evidence use, counterargument, mechanics. Four performance levels."`
- `"Suggest three differentiated activities for [concept]: one hands-on, one text-based, one collaborative."`

**Tips:**
- Always review AI-generated content for accuracy — especially dates, statistics, and citations.
- Use AI output as a starting draft, not a final product. Your subject-matter expertise and knowledge of your students is irreplaceable.
- Save strong prompts in a personal prompt library for reuse.

---

### Differentiation & Personalization

AI can rapidly adapt the same content to different reading levels, learning modalities, or student needs.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Rewrite this paragraph at a 5th-grade reading level without losing the key ideas: [paste text]"`
- `"Translate this explanation of [concept] into an analogy a middle schooler would find relatable."`
- `"Create a visual/spatial version of this concept map for [topic]."`
- `"Generate three versions of this problem: easy, medium, and challenging."`

---

### Assessment & Feedback

AI can speed up formative feedback, help design varied assessments, and reduce time spent on first-pass grading.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Review this student paragraph and give structured feedback on thesis, evidence, and clarity. Do not rewrite it — only give feedback."`
- `"Generate 20 multiple-choice questions on [chapter/topic]. Include one clearly correct answer and three plausible distractors each."`
- `"Create a self-assessment checklist students can use before submitting their essay."`

**Tips:**
- Be explicit that you want feedback, not rewrites — otherwise AI will rewrite student work for you.
- Use AI-generated questions as a starting pool; edit for alignment with your specific learning objectives.

---

### Classroom Discussion Prompts

AI is useful for generating Socratic questions, devil's advocate positions, and scenario-based discussion starters.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Generate a Socratic seminar starter question for [topic] that has no single correct answer."`
- `"What are the strongest counterarguments to [position]? Write them as a student might present them in a debate."`
- `"Create a realistic case study involving [concept] that would prompt ethical discussion in a high school class."`

## For Students

### Research & Source Evaluation

AI is useful for understanding a topic quickly, but it cannot replace verified sources. Treat AI output as a starting point, not a citation.

**Practical uses:**
- Get a fast overview: `"Explain [topic] at a high school level. What are the key debates or open questions?"`
- Identify search terms: `"What keywords should I use to search academic databases for research on [topic]?"`
- Understand a complex source: `"Summarize the main argument of this passage: [paste text]"`

**Critical rules:**
- **Never cite AI directly** as a source. It does not have access to verified data and can hallucinate facts, dates, and citations.
- Verify any specific claim (statistics, names, quotes) in a primary or peer-reviewed source before using it.
- Use [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com), your library database, or [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org) for real citations.

### Writing Assistance

AI is strongest as a thinking partner and revision tool, not a ghostwriter. Work produced entirely by AI is not yours and typically shows it.

**Use AI to:**
- **Unstick your writing:** `"I'm writing about [topic] and I'm stuck after this paragraph: [paste]. What are three ways I could continue?"`
- **Get feedback on a draft:** `"Give me specific feedback on the clarity and argument structure of this paragraph: [paste]. Don't rewrite it."`
- **Improve phrasing:** `"This sentence is awkward: [paste]. Give me three alternative versions that keep the same meaning."`
- **Check logic:** `"Is my reasoning in this argument sound? Where is it weakest? [paste argument]"`

**Avoid:**
- Asking AI to write entire sections or essays for you. Beyond academic integrity issues, you miss the actual learning.
- Accepting AI phrasing wholesale — it flattens your voice and introduces a generic tone.

---

### Concept Explanation & Tutoring

AI is an infinitely patient tutor available at any hour. Use it to get unstuck on concepts, see alternative explanations, or check your understanding.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Explain [concept] like I'm a high school student who hasn't seen it before."`
- `"I think [concept] works like [your understanding]. Is that right? What am I missing?"`
- `"Give me an analogy for [abstract concept] that connects to everyday life."`
- `"I'm confused about [specific step] in [math/science topic]. Walk me through it step by step."`
- `"Quiz me on [topic] with 5 questions, then tell me which answers I got wrong and why."`

---

### Study Strategies

AI can help you structure your study sessions, create practice materials, and explain mistakes.

**Practical prompts:**
- `"Create a 5-question practice quiz on [topic] with answer explanations."`
- `"I'm studying for an exam on [topic]. What are the most important concepts I should focus on?"`
- `"Make a set of flashcard prompts for the key terms in this passage: [paste]"`
- `"Explain why my answer was wrong: I answered [your answer] but the correct answer is [correct answer]."`

**Spaced repetition tip:** Use AI to generate question sets, then load them into a free tool like [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) or [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com) for long-term retention practice.

## Responsible AI Use

### Academic Integrity

Check your institution's AI policy before using AI on any graded work. Policies vary widely:
- Some allow AI for brainstorming only.
- Some allow AI-assisted drafting with disclosure.
- Some prohibit AI entirely.

When in doubt, ask your instructor. Submitting AI-generated content as your own — without permission — is academic dishonesty at most institutions.

### AI Limitations and Hallucinations

AI language models are confident but not always accurate. They can:
- State false facts with high confidence ("hallucinate")
- Fabricate citations that look real but don't exist
- Reflect biases present in training data
- Have a knowledge cutoff date and miss recent developments

**Always verify** specific facts, figures, dates, quotes, and citations in authoritative sources before relying on them.

### Critical Thinking with AI Output

- Treat AI answers as one perspective, not ground truth.
- Ask "why" — `"Why is that the case?"` forces the model to show its reasoning, where weaknesses often surface.
- Ask for counterarguments: `"What would someone who disagrees with this say?"`
- Cross-check claims with multiple sources.

### Privacy and Data

- Do not paste personally identifiable information (student names, grades, IDs) into public AI tools.
- Be aware that conversations with commercial AI tools may be used to train future models — check the privacy policy of your tool.
- Schools and districts with data privacy obligations (FERPA, COPPA, GDPR) should use enterprise or compliant versions of AI tools.
